# What teams actually plan — the revealed-target distribution

*(n=1,801 graduated coins with an observed team exit; gated membership; sustained
prices only — a level needs ≥3 prints to count. Point-in-time, leak-audited.)*

The team's **revealed target** = the highest sustained multiple they allow before
their first sell. It answers "what did the team want to get it to?" with behavior,
not guesses:

| revealed target | share of teams |
|---|---|
| < 1.2× (instant dump) | **69%** |
| 1.2× – 2× (scalp the graduation pop) | 23% |
| ≥ 2× (actually run it) | **8.6%** |

Median: **1.07×**. p90: 1.87×. p95: 2.80×.

Read plainly: **the modal Pump.fun team's entire plan is the graduation moment
itself.** They accumulate on the curve, graduation provides the liquidity event, they
exit at par-and-change within minutes (median first sell 2.4 min, leading the collapse
80% of the time). "Running it up first" is a 1-in-12 behavior, faintly predictable at
T+0 (ROC 0.668) but not tradeably so (NEGATIVE_RESULTS #9).

Implications:
- The **exit alarm and pre-warning are correctly aimed** — for two thirds of coins the
  team's decision precedes everything else that matters.
- The crowd, not the team, produces the upside: 43% of coins sustain a 2× at some
  point, but 80% of those do it through/after the team's selling.
- Any product claim should be about **when the team leaves**, never about where the
  coin can go. The first is measured and predictable; the second is not.
